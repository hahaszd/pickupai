# PickupAI — Core GTM Playbook (Greater Sydney Plumbers)

This playbook is a practical, step-by-step GTM guide for launching the **Core (no platform integration)** AI receptionist product to **Greater Sydney plumbers**.

## 0) What we’re selling (Core)
**Outcome promise (no booking / no integrations):**
> We answer every call 24/7 and turn it into a structured SMS lead. During business hours, genuine emergencies are warm-transferred to the owner; after-hours, callers get instant SMS confirmation and a clear callback commitment.

**Pricing reference:** see `docs/core-pricing-gtm.md`.

---

## 1) Recommended GTM strategy (best for a solo founder)
The fastest path to revenue is **manual outbound** + **a simple demo** + **a paid pilot**.

### Why this is the best first move
- Your product’s ROI is immediately understandable (missed calls → missed jobs).
- Trades owners are reachable by phone/SMS and decide quickly.
- You can get real call data fast and improve scripts/urgency rules.

### What to avoid early
- Big-budget ads before you have conversion proof and 2–3 case studies.
- Complicated multi-channel funnels. Win with one channel first.

---

## 2) Target customer profile (who to sell to first)
Prioritise businesses with the strongest “missed call pain”:
- **Solo / small crew (1–5 people)**
- Mostly **inbound phone leads**
- On tools all day, or frequently driving
- Works **after-hours emergency callouts**
- Has reviews mentioning “couldn’t get through / no answer / slow response” (strong signal)

### Disqualifiers (don’t waste time early)
- They already have full-time admin answering every call.
- They refuse to forward calls / refuse any automation.
- They only want “free trial” but won’t commit to configuring postcodes/hours.

---

## 3) Channel strategy (what to do in which stage)

### Stage 1 (Week 1–2): Direct outreach (primary)
Goal: **2–3 paid pilots** (or 10 demos booked).

**Best sources for lists:**
- Google Maps (Sydney plumber) + their website contact details
- Yellow Pages
- Local suburb-specific searches (e.g., “emergency plumber {suburb}”)

**Best outreach order:**
1) SMS
2) Phone call (same day or next morning)
3) Email (supporting details + demo link)

### Stage 2 (Week 3–6): Referral loop + local proof (secondary)
Goal: predictable pipeline from referrals.
- Ask every satisfied pilot customer for **2 tradie referrals**
- Offer a simple referral incentive (e.g., “1 month $50 off”)

### Stage 3 (After 2–3 case studies): Small paid ads (optional)
Goal: scale after you know your conversion rate.
- Start small ($20–$50/day), target plumbing/electrical business owners in Greater Sydney
- Ads should drive to a **demo number** or **book-a-call** page

---

## 4) Your minimum assets (what you need before outreach)
You do not need a fancy brand. You need credibility and speed.

### Asset checklist
- **1-page landing page** (or Notion/Carrd) with:
  - one-sentence outcome promise
  - 3 bullet benefits
  - pricing (Core)
  - “Book a 10-min call” + “Request demo number”
- **A demo phone number** (that shows the flow end-to-end)
- **One screenshot example** of the SMS summary (mocked is fine initially)
- **Pilot offer** (paid, simple): “7 days, cancel anytime, go live in 24–48h”

---

## 5) Outreach scripts (copy/paste)

### 5.1 SMS #1 (cold, short)
> Hey {Name} — I’m local near Sydney and building PickupAI for plumbers. It answers missed calls 24/7, asks the right questions, and texts you a structured lead summary. During business hours, real emergencies can warm-transfer to you. Want to test a demo number?

### 5.2 SMS #2 (if no reply in 24h)
> Quick one — do you miss calls while on the tools? If yes, I can set you up with a 7‑day pilot this week (no admin work, just call forwarding). Want me to send details?

### 5.3 Phone opener (10–20 seconds)
> Hey {Name}, I’ll be super quick — I’m building an AI receptionist for Sydney plumbers. It picks up every call, captures the job details, and texts you a clean summary. If it’s an emergency during business hours it can warm-transfer to you. Are missed calls a problem for you right now?

### 5.4 Qualifying questions (keep it to 3)
1) Roughly how many calls per day/week?
2) Do you do after-hours emergency callouts?
3) Are you okay to enable “no-answer call forwarding” after ~20 seconds?

### 5.5 Close (pilot)
> Let’s do a 7‑day pilot. If it doesn’t capture extra jobs, cancel. If it does, you keep it. We can go live in 24–48 hours.

### 5.6 Objection handling (common)
**“Will customers hate AI?”**
> It’s designed to be fast and helpful. Most callers just want someone to pick up and confirm you’ll call back. We also warm-transfer genuine emergencies during business hours.

**“I don’t want extra admin.”**
> No admin. You just set call forwarding once. You get a text summary after each call. That’s it.

**“I’m already busy.”**
> That’s exactly why it helps. It captures the lead while you’re busy, and you call back when free — with the details already in your SMS.

## 6) Pilot offer structure (recommended)
Keep it simple and time-boxed.

### Pilot offer (suggested)
- 7 days, **paid pilot** (e.g., $99 credited to first month) OR founding offer pricing
- Go-live in 24–48 hours
- Clear success criteria:
  - every call answered
  - every call produces an SMS lead summary
  - emergencies during business hours are warm-transferred

### What you need from the customer to start
- business name
- owner mobile number (for SMS + warm transfer)
- service postcode list
- business hours (use B1 defaults if unsure)
- callback promise window defaults

---

## 7) Tracking (don’t skip this)
Set up a simple spreadsheet (Google Sheets) with:
- Company name
- Suburb
- Phone
- Website
- Owner name (if known)
- Status: contacted / replied / demo booked / pilot / paying / churned
- Notes: objections, call volume, after-hours yes/no
- Next follow-up date

### Daily targets (realistic solo founder)
- 20–30 SMS/day
- 5–10 calls/day (follow-ups)
- 2 demos/week minimum

---

## 8) What “good” looks like (success metrics)
Use these as stop/go signals.

### Week 1–2
- 50 contacts → 10 replies → 5 demos → 2 paid pilots

### First 30 days
- 10 paying customers OR clear evidence you need to change positioning/offer

### Key operational metrics
- Time to onboard < 60–90 min per customer
- Pilot → paid conversion > 50%
- Churn in first 60 days < 20% (early target)

---

## 9) Iteration loops (how to improve fast)
After each pilot week, update:
- intake question order (postcode first)
- emergency keyword triggers
- SMS summary format (shorter, more structured)
- after-hours promise wording

Keep a simple “Top 10 reasons leads were bad” list and fix those first.
