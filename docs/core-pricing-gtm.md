# Core Pricing & GTM (Sydney Plumbers) — v1

This document defines the **Core (no platform integration)** packaging and pricing for the AI receptionist product targeting **Greater Sydney plumbers**.

## 1) Product scope (Core only)

### What Core does (what you can promise)
- **24/7 full call answering** (no missed calls).
- **Plumber intake script** that captures key details in one pass (incl. **postcode first**).
- **Configurable callback promise** (business chooses the commitment window).
- **SMS to customer**: confirmation + callback promise + request for address/photos if needed.
- **SMS to owner**: structured lead summary + urgency label + callback number.
- **Business-hours emergency warm transfer** (A2):
  - **Mon–Fri 07:00–17:00; Sat 08:00–12:00**: emergency calls are summarised + warm-transferred to owner.
  - After-hours: default is **no transfer**, SMS summary + callback promise. “Extreme emergency transfer” can be enabled.
- **Service area gating via postcodes (C1)**: only accept (or prioritise) leads inside a configurable postcode list; out-of-area calls are handled politely with a minimal lead capture.

### What Core explicitly does NOT do (avoid churn)
- No “auto booking a job into your calendar” promise.
- No “create jobs/leads inside ServiceM8/simPRO/AroFlo/Tradify/Fergus”.
- No full CRM/dispatch/job management portal (beyond basic summaries/logs).

## 2) Competitive pricing anchors (public AU pages)
- **TradieDesk**: **$200/mo + $400 one-time setup** (includes booking/calendar + broader workflow).
- **LANA (base)**: **$300/mo + $1,000 training** (higher tiers add job creation / deeper integration).
- **Siptalk**: **$0.275/min**, no setup (commodity per-minute alternative).

Implication: Core should win on **lower upfront friction + plumber-specific emergency handling**, not on “more features than a full workflow suite”.

## 3) Recommended pricing (Core)

### Public plan (simple, easy to sell)
**Core Plan: AUD $199 / month**
- Month-to-month (cancel anytime).
- Includes 1 business number (forwarding), 1 owner transfer number, customer+owner SMS, postcode list, A2+B1 emergency policy, basic summaries.

**Fair-use line (keep it simple):**
> Designed for typical small trade call volumes. Extremely high call volumes may require a custom plan.

### Founding customer offer (to manufacture price advantage)
For the first **10–20 paying customers**:
- **$149 / month for 3 months**, then rolls to **$199 / month**.

Optional close-boost (use sparingly):
- “Lock $149/mo for 12 months” if you need an irresistible early-adopter hook.

## 4) Setup fee policy (best for your current stage)

### Default for GTM weeks 1–6: $0 setup
Rationale:
- You need speed, volume, and learning loops.
- Your Core onboarding is lightweight (postcodes + hours + callback promise + emergency rules).
- Removing setup friction increases conversion for cold outreach.

### When to introduce setup fees later (objective triggers)
Introduce either **$199 setup** OR **$0 setup with a 2–3 month minimum** when any of these is true:
- You have **10 paying customers** (or 30 days of learnings), and
- Onboarding time is consistently < **60–90 minutes** per customer, and/or
- You’re getting too many low-intent leads wasting your time.

## 5) Optional add-ons (keep off the main pricing page)
Only add these if you see clear demand:
- **Extra owner transfer number**: +$19–$29/mo
- **Extra business line/second number**: +$29–$49/mo

## 6) Offer framing (copy you can reuse)

### One-sentence outcome promise
> “We answer every call 24/7 and turn it into a structured SMS lead. During business hours, genuine emergencies are warm-transferred to the owner; after-hours, callers get instant SMS confirmation and a clear callback commitment.”

### Three bullets (homepage-ready)
- **Never miss another call** (24/7 full coverage).
- **Emergency calls handled properly** (business-hours warm transfer; after-hours rules).
- **Every call becomes a usable lead** (postcode, issue, urgency, best callback, optional media request).

## 7) Pricing A/B tests (first 2–3 weeks)
Run small tests to find the best conversion + customer quality:
- **Price**: $179 vs $199 (same product).
- **Offer framing**: “$0 setup” vs “$99 for 7 days credited to first month”.

## 8) Metrics to decide price changes
- **Activation**: onboarded & taking calls within 48 hours.
- **Conversion**: outreach → demo → paid.
- **Founder time per customer**: target < 1–2h once playbook stabilises.
- **Early churn**: cancellations within 60 days (log reasons).
- **Call volume distribution**: identify top 10% heavy users (to decide if you need a “high-volume” tier).
